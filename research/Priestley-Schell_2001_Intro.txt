The Darwin Information Typing Architecture (DITA) is an XML-based, end-to-end architecture for authoring, producing, and delivering techni cal information. This architecture consists of a set of design principles for creating "information-typed" modules at a topic level and for using that contednt in deliveryu modes such as online help and product support portals on the Web."

The extensible architecture, howwever, is the defining part of this design for technical information.

Benefits of DITA architecture.
- Encapsulation
- Polymorphism
- Message passing
